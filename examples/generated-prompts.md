# Example world prompts

These shortened examples illustrate different spatial topologies available to the development fixtures. The app produces longer prompts plus a structured scene plan, confidence object, evidence weights, and limitations.

## 1. Known lyrical work — looping topology

Create a coherent explorable environment carved into a pale threshold-city around a luminous inner sanctuary. Organize it as two connected circuits that leave and return to the same shared court. One circuit passes behind limestone measuring walls; the other crosses translucent mineral rooms that reveal the sanctuary from changing angles. Returning to the court should make its familiar surfaces feel newly legible rather than serving as a conventional ending. Preserve human-readable routes, consistent scale, and intermittent orientation views.

## 2. Obscure lyrical song — branching topology

Build one navigable botanical-industrial site where glass-bearing trees grow through an abandoned precision works. From a shared maintenance court, let an orchard route and a machine route diverge, reveal contrasting evidence, and reconnect beside a silent stamping press. Neither branch is the designated climax. Use living wood, smoked glass, dark steel, and amber resin consistently, making the organic and mechanical systems physically interdependent.

## 3. Unknown instrumental — radial topology

Create a vast explorable tidal plain made of resonant stone, shallow reflective channels, and suspended metallic spans. Distribute dense rhythmic repetition around a central commons with a navigable perimeter ring and one offset quiet chamber. Express intensity through the spacing and overlap of bridges rather than making one area automatically taller or larger. Use the horizon, radial seams, and recurring resonance patterns for orientation from every route.

## Shared negative prompt

No text, lyrics, signage, logos, recognizable people, living-artist style imitation, trademarked fictional settings, music-video recreation, disconnected dioramas, single-view composition, impossible non-navigable geometry, excessive unrelated symbols, or assumed timed animation.
